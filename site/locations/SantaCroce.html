Architectural style: Italian Gothic

Artworks

Tomb of Carlo Marsuppini

Marble

Monument tomb created by the stonecutter Desiderio da Settignano for the humanist scholar and Chancellor of Florence Carlo Marsuppini.

St. Sylvester Sealing the Dragon's Mouth

Fresco

Full title: "St. Sylvester sealing the dragon's mouth and resuscitating two pagan magicians." Created by Maso di Banco, who was an assistant of Giotto.

St. Francis Undergoing the Test by Fire

Fresco

Depicts St. Francis undergoing the test by fire before the sultan of Egypt. Contains one of the earliest known representations of black people in Western art.

Legend of the True Cross

Fresco 1385–1387

Fresco cycle in the apse of Santa Croce. Agnolo Gaddi was the last major Florentine painter stylistically descended from Giotto.

Last Supper with the Tree of Life

Fresco

Frescoes in the Refectory of Santa Croce. Notable for placing Judas on "our" side of the table, a convention that continued until Leonardo's Last Supper.

Funeral of St. Francis

Fresco

Notable for its calm composition balanced with dramatic details, exemplifying Giotto's narrative mastery.

Baroncelli Chapel Frescoes

Fresco c. 1328–1330

Frescoes in the Baroncelli Chapel by Bernardo Daddi, a follower of Giotto.
